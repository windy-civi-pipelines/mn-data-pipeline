No. 39, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 7, 2025 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2recognizing Kevin Cher Chai Vang and his contributions to the Hmong 1.3community. 1.4WHEREAS, Kevin Cher Chai Vang was born on June 5, 1963, in Nabou Thanh Ta, Laos, 1.5to Chong Neng Vang and See Vue; and 1.6WHEREAS, after enduring profound loss, Kevin and his siblings sought refuge in Thailand, 1.7where he met his future wife, Mai Yee Yang. They, along with their three children, immigrated to 1.8the United States in 1987, first settling in Milwaukee and later moving to Minnesota, where they 1.9welcomed two more daughters. Their family has since blossomed into five accomplished adults 1.10and 20 grandchildren; and 1.11WHEREAS, Kevin earned his GED and machinist certificate from Anoka Technical College. 1.12With help from his wife, Mai Yee, he co-founded several businesses, including the Asian American 1.13Foods Store, Asian American Temporary Services, Vang Auto Sales, and the Hmong Village 1.14Shopping Center; and 1.15WHEREAS, Kevin has served the Hmong community for over three decades, including as 1.16treasurer of the Vang clan, president of the Hmong 18 Council of Minnesota, and president of the 1.17Hmong 18 Council Coordination of America. He mastered traditional funeral rites under esteemed 1.18mentors and was recognized as a Txiv Xaiv, training hundreds of students and providing comfort 1.19to countless families through ritual and ceremony; and 1.20WHEREAS, Kevin was supported throughout his journey by his wife, Mai Yee, who cared 1.21for their young family in the refugee camp with determination and her skill in Hmong pajntaub. 1.22She later studied embroidery and alterations, worked full time from 1991 to 2006, and eventually 1.23took on key roles in the family businesses managing payroll, recruiting, and finances with steady 1.24leadership and grace; and 1.25WHEREAS, Kevin and Mai Yee are being honored for their lifetime achievement and 45th 1.26anniversary celebration; and 1.27NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 1.28commends Kevin Cher Chai Vang on his efforts advocating for Hmong culture. 1.29BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an 1.30enrolled copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the 1.31Chair of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to Kevin Cher Chai Vang 1.32and Mai Yee Yang Vang.
