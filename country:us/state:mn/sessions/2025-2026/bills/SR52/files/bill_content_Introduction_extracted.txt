No. 52, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 19, 2025 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2recognizing Rick Evans on the occasion of his retirement. 1.3WHEREAS, those who work tirelessly on behalf of the state perform a service worthy of 1.4legislative acknowledgment; and 1.5WHEREAS, Rick Evans was born in Topeka, Kansas, on August 6, 1954, and moved to 1.6Hopkins, Minnesota, in 1968. As an undergraduate at the University of Minnesota, Rick was elected 1.7student body president. He earned a bachelor's degree in political science and served as an intern 1.8to Senators Bob Brown and John Keefe; and 1.9WHEREAS, Rick began his career in state politics in 1976, as the legislative campaign 1.10coordinator for Minnesota's Independent-Republican Party. He went on to join Dave Durenberger's 1.11staff for both his 1977 gubernatorial and 1978 United States Senate campaigns; and 1.12WHEREAS, after graduating from the University of Minnesota Law School in 1981, Rick 1.13clerked with the Hennepin County Attorney's Office and practiced civil commercial litigation. 1.14Throughout the 1980's, he was a leader in the state Republican Party, serving as its vice-chairman 1.15and later its treasurer; and 1.16WHEREAS, in the 1990's, he moved to the Washington, D.C., area to serve as Senator 1.17Durenberger's chief of staff. Upon his return to Minneapolis, he resumed his legal practice along 1.18with his involvement in state politics. In 2000, he served as state campaign chair for Senator Rod 1.19Grams, and in 2002 he joined Xcel Energy, where he oversaw government relations. From 1999 1.20to 2012, he volunteered as a leader of Boy Scout Troop 123 in Edina; and 1.21WHEREAS, after decades of hard work and public service, Rick can look forward to enjoying 1.22his retirement with his wife and their three children; and 1.23NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 1.24thanks Rick Evans for his service to the state and extends its best wishes for his retirement. 2.1BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an 2.2enrolled copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the 2.3Chair of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to Rick Evans.
