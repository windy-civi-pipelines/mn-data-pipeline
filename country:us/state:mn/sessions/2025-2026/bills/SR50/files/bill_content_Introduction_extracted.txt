No. 50, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 17, 2025 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2honoring the memory of David J. Jergenson. 1.3WHEREAS, David J. Jergenson was born January 1, 1918, to Laura and George Jergensen 1.4of Donnelly, Minnesota. The seventh of 11 children, he farmed with his dad until he was drafted 1.5into the United States Army in 1942; and 1.6WHEREAS, David served honorably in the United States Army during World War II in 1.7Algeria and Italy. In 1945, he was honorably discharged with the rank of Tech Sergeant; and 1.8WHEREAS, after exiting the service, David returned to Donnelly and married Julette 1.9Gilbertson on March 10, 1946. In 1948, they purchased a farm north of Cyrus and farmed and began 1.10raising mink. David continued helping his son, Duane, on the mink ranch long after his retirement 1.11in 1982, and in 2012, David was honored with the title "Fur Rancher of the Year" by the Minnesota 1.12Fur Breeders Association; and 1.13WHEREAS, David and Julette began spending winters in Mesa, Arizona, in 1982, where 1.14they enjoyed spending time with family and new friends, singing, and playing pool and dominoes. 1.15They enjoyed 34 winters in Mesa. In his later years, David enjoyed spending time at the VFW in 1.16Alexandria, where he would bring his own hot chocolate rather than drink their coffee. David loved 1.17the comradery of the VFW; and 1.18WHEREAS, David, after living a long life as a valued member of his community, passed 1.19away peacefully on March 14, 2025, at the age of 107; and 1.20NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 1.21honors the memory of David J. Jergenson, acknowledges his military service to this nation and his 1.22contributions to his community, and extends condolences to his family and friends. 1.23BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an 1.24enrolled copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the 1.25Chair of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to the family of David J. 1.26Jergenson.
