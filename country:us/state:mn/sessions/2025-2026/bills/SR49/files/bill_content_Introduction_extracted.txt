No. 49, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 17, 2025 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2honoring Linda Wallenberg for being named Minnesota's 2025 Teacher of the Year. 1.3WHEREAS, the Minnesota Teacher of the Year program is the oldest and most prestigious recognition 1.4program in Minnesota to honor excellence in education; and 1.5WHEREAS, Linda Wallenberg earned a degree in English and Scandinavian Studies from Gustavus 1.6Adolphus College in 1975, after studying abroad at Uppsala University in Sweden; and 1.7WHEREAS, Linda has been an English teacher for nearly 50 years. After teaching eighth-grade 1.8English in Faribault, she started a new position at Eden Prairie High School in 1977 and has remained there 1.9ever since; and 1.10WHEREAS, Linda later became the first certified Swedish teacher in Minnesota and earned a master's 1.11in English education from the University of Minnesota; and 1.12WHEREAS, Linda has taught in Canterbury, England, and for 42 summers has served as a teacher 1.13and director at Concordia College's Swedish Language Village; and 1.14WHEREAS, in 1988, Linda received the Royal Order of the Polar Star from His Majesty King Carl 1.15XVI Gustaf of Sweden for her work in Swedish language education; and 1.16WHEREAS, Linda was named Eden Prairie Teacher of the Year three times and was previously a 1.17finalist for Minnesota Teacher of the Year in 2005; and 1.18WHEREAS, Linda's students have gone on to be professional poets, professional hockey players, 1.19and Olympic medalists. In the past five years, at least three Minnesota Teacher of the Year finalists have 1.20described her as their inspiration; and 1.21WHEREAS, Linda coached the Eden Prairie high school gymnastics team to a 150-7 record and two 1.22national titles. She was twice named National Gymnastics Coach of the Year and was inducted into the 1.23Minnesota Gymnastics Hall of Fame in 2005; and 1.24WHEREAS, Linda is also the author of two books, "Fundamental Gymnastics" and "Play-By-Play 1.25Gymnastics"; and 1.26WHEREAS, Linda, selected from among 142 nominees across the state, is the 61st recipient of the 1.27prestigious award and the first from the Eden Prairie district. By winning this honor, she will represent the 1.28profession and serve as an advocate for education and spokesperson for teachers statewide; and 1.29NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it thanks 1.30and commends Linda Wallenberg for her dedication to teaching and to her students and congratulates her 1.31for being named Minnesota's 2025 Teacher of the Year. 1.32BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an enrolled 1.33copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the Chair of the Senate 1.34Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to Linda Wallenberg. Tom BotternSecretary of the Senate Erin P. MurphyChair, Senate Committee onRules and Administration Steve A.
