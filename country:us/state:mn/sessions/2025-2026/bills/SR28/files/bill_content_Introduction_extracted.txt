No. 28, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on April 7, 2025 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2recognizing Bret Heitkamp on the occasion of his retirement. 1.3WHEREAS, those who work tirelessly in their communities perform a service worthy of 1.4legislative acknowledgment; and 1.5WHEREAS, Bret Heitkamp grew up in Adrian, Minnesota, and attended Mankato State 1.6University. After receiving his master's degree in Public Administration from Hamline University, 1.7he served as the parks and recreation director in Champlin, Minnesota, from 1986 until his promotion 1.8to city administrator in 1999; and 1.9WHEREAS, when Bret's tenure began, there were relatively few developments along Highway 1.10169 in Champlin. Thanks in no small part to his efforts, the city has grown to include numerous 1.11commercial and residential developments, including the Elm Creek Commerce Center, Champlin 1.12Marketplace, South Pond Center, Elm Creek Neighborhoods, Emery Village, and more. Over the 1.13same period, Champlin's population has grown by more than 10,000 people; and 1.14WHEREAS, Bret oversaw the development of the Mississippi Crossings Event Center and 1.15Outdoor Performing area and assisted in leveraging over $30,000,000 in noncity public investment 1.16toward improvements to Highway 169, Mill Pond, Mill Pond Dam, Chandler Park, and Mississippi 1.17Pointe Park; and 1.18WHEREAS, Bret's work on behalf of his community includes serving on the Anoka-Champlin 1.19Fire Board, the Quad Cities Cable Communications Board, the Ice Arena Task Force, and the 1.20Mississippi Crossings Task Force, as well as overseeing the Municipal Facilities Expansion Project; 1.21and 1.22WHEREAS, in May 2025, Bret will embark on a well-deserved retirement; and 1.23NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 1.24thanks Bret Heitkamp for his career in public service. 1.25BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an 1.26enrolled copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the 1.27Chair of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to Bret Heitkamp.
