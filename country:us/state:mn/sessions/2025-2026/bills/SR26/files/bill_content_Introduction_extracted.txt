No. 26, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on April 7, 2025 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2honoring the Civic League Day Nursery. 1.3WHEREAS, the Civic League Day Nursery is celebrating its 95th anniversary as the 1.4longest-running nonprofit child care center in Minnesota; and 1.5WHEREAS, child care is crucial in having a thriving community by providing essential 1.6services that most families in the Rochester area need; and 1.7WHEREAS, the Civic League's passion for child care has been recognized with its 1.8commitment to nurturing young children in a safe, supportive environment while ensuring families 1.9have access to dependable care, preparing children for future success, and supporting working 1.10parents; and 1.11WHEREAS, the Civic League has prioritized accessibility and inclusion, ensuring that 1.12families have access to quality early education regardless of income level; and 1.13WHEREAS, the Civic League's focus on whole-child development, emphasizing social, 1.14emotional, and academic growth to prepare children for lifelong learning, is a unique component 1.15of its organization; and 1.16WHEREAS, the leadership that the Civic League Day Nursery is taking is admirable, 1.17advocating in its role as a trusted community organization and further amplifying its contributions 1.18to child care in the Rochester area; and 1.19NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that 1.20heartfelt congratulations and gratitude are extended to the Civic League Day Nursery for its 1.21extraordinary service and well-deserved recognition as the longest-running nonprofit child care 1.22center. 1.23BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an 1.24enrolled copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the 1.25Chair of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to Jackie Benoit-Petrich 1.26on the behalf of the Civic League Day Nursery as a lasting tribute to its remarkable achievements. Tom BotternSecretary of the Senate Erin P. MurphyChair, Senate Committee onRules and Administration Carla J.
