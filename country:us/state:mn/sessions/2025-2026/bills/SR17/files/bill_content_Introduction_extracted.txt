No. 17, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on March 10, 2025 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2expressing the sense of the Senate condemning Governor Tim Walz's recent comments 1.3to the Minnesota Nursing Association. 1.4WHEREAS, while speaking at an event with the Minnesota Nurses Association on February 1.525, 2025, Governor Tim Walz stated, "I see the pundits on TV, 'What's wrong with the Democratic 1.6Party?' What's wrong is our country is being stolen by fascists and Nazis"; and 1.7WHEREAS, flippant use of the term Nazi to refer to political opponents is particularly heinous 1.8for its comparison to Germany's Nazi Party, which was responsible for launching World War II 1.9and the Holocaust, leading to the deaths of more than 6,000,000 Jewish people in concentration 1.10camps; and 1.11WHEREAS, using charged political rhetoric further divides our state and hinders our ability 1.12to view each other as neighbors, not enemies; and 1.13WHEREAS, Governor Walz's remarks referring to our country being stolen imply that he 1.14views the election as invalid, serving to reduce confidence in the American electoral process; NOW, 1.15THEREFORE, 1.16BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it condemns Governor Tim 1.17Walz for his remarks on February 25, 2025, referring to political opponents as fascists and Nazis. 1.18BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Senate of the State of Minnesota calls on Governor 1.19Tim Walz to apologize to members of the Jewish community of Minnesota for devaluing the memory 1.20of those lost during the Holocaust. 1.21BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Senate of the State of Minnesota further calls on 1.22Governor Tim Walz to apologize to the public for using divisive and heinous rhetoric to describe 1.23his political opponents. 2.1BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare a copy 2.2of this resolution, to be authenticated by his signature and that of the Chair of the Senate Rules and 2.3Administration Committee, and transmit it to Governor Tim Walz.
