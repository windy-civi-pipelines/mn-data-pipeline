No. 22, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on April 1, 2025 1.1A Senate resolution 1.2honoring the life and memory of Thomas Zabinski. 1.3WHEREAS, those who work tirelessly in their communities to protect the public's safety 1.4and well-being perform an important service deserving of special legislative recognition; and 1.5WHEREAS, Thomas "Tom" Zabinski was born October 13, 1963, in St. Cloud, Minnesota, 1.6to Joseph Jr. and Joan Zabinski. Tom attended Technical Senior High School in St. Cloud and 1.7earned an associate's degree from Dakota County Technical College in the Business Management 1.8and Supervisory Leadership program. On April 30, 1988, he married Diane Jurek at St. Elizabeth 1.9Catholic Church in Brennyville; and 1.10WHEREAS, Tom began his lifelong career in public service in 1985 with the St. Cloud 1.11Township Public Works Department. He was a dedicated member of the St. Cloud Township Fire 1.12Department, where he served as acting fire chief; the St. Cloud Fire and Rescue Department; and 1.13the community at large. He was a member of the Eagles Club, Aerie 622, and the Knights of 1.14Columbus. Through hard work and dedication, he was promoted to Public Works Operations 1.15Manager for the city of St. Cloud in 2010; and 1.16WHEREAS, Tom was a devoted fan of Minnesota sports, including the Vikings and the 1.17Wild, and he also enjoyed deer hunting, fishing, bowling, golf, and an occasional trip to the casino. 1.18He loved to camp with his family, spend time with this grandchildren, and share his favorite vanilla 1.19ice cream with his cat, Layla. His friends and family will remember him as a true "do-it-yourselfer" 1.20and will miss his jokes and humor; and 1.21WHEREAS, Tom passed away on March 11, 2025. He is survived by Diane; children 1.22Courtney and Brooke; grandchild Teagan; brothers James, Steven, and Gary; and sisters Barbara, 1.23Janice, and Katherine; and 1.24NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 1.25commemorates the life and service of Thomas Zabinski and extends its sincere condolences to his 1.26family and all who knew him. 1.27BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to prepare an 1.28enrolled copy of this resolution, to be authenticated by the Secretary's signature and that of the 1.29Chair of the Senate Rules and Administration Committee, and transmit it to the family of Thomas 1.30Zabinski.
