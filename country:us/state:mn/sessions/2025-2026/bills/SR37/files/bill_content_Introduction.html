S.R. No. 37, as introduced: 94th Legislative Session (2025-2026) Posted on May 5, 2025




1.1A Senate resolution

1.2reaffirming its commitment to the strengthening and deepening of the sister 

1.3ties between the State of Minnesota and Taiwan; reaffirming its support for 

1.4the Taiwan Relations Act by urging all enterprises in Minnesota to refer to 

1.5Taiwan as Taiwan; supporting Taiwan's signing of a Bilateral Trade 

1.6Agreement, the Avoidance of Double Taxation Agreement, and the 

1.7Indo-Pacific Economic Framework for Prosperity with the United States; 

1.8and reiterating continued support for increasing Taiwan's international profile.

1.9WHEREAS, the State of Minnesota established a sister-state relationship with Taiwan in 

1.101984 and Minnesota and Taiwan have enjoyed strong bilateral trade, educational and
         cultural 

1.11exchanges, and tourism; and

1.12WHEREAS, Taiwan shares the same values of freedom, democracy, human rights, the rule 

1.13of law, peace, and prosperity with the United States and the State of Minnesota; and

1.14WHEREAS, the United States ranks as Taiwan's second-largest trading partner. Taiwan is 

1.15the eighth-largest trading partner of the United States, and bilateral trade reached
         more than $127.5 

1.16billion in 2023; and

1.17WHEREAS, Taiwan imported $3.8 billion worth of United States farm products in 2024, 

1.18making it the fourth-largest market on a per capita basis among the top ten United
         States agricultural 

1.19export destinations and the eighth-largest market for United States soybeans and corn;
         and

1.20WHEREAS, due to its high-quality produce, the United States remains one of Taiwan's 

1.21largest sources of agricultural products, supplying more than one-fifth of the country's
         major 

1.22agricultural imports; and

1.23WHEREAS, Taiwan and the State of Minnesota have enjoyed a long and mutually beneficial 

1.24relationship with the prospect of future growth. Taiwan was Minnesota's ninth-largest
         export market 

1.25in 2024, with $644 million worth of Minnesota goods exported to Taiwan; and

1.26WHEREAS, to strengthen the Taiwan-Minnesota bilateral economic relationship, it is 

1.27essential to support Minnesota businesses to enhance their economic engagement with
         Taiwan 

1.28based on the 1979 Taiwan Relations Act, Public Law 96-8, United States Code, title
         22, section 

1.293301. As article 4, section b, of the 1979 Taiwan Relations Act stipulates that "wherever
         the laws 

1.30of the United States refer or relate to foreign countries, nations, states, governments,
         or similar 

1.31entities, such terms shall include and such laws shall apply with respect to Taiwan,"
         it is legitimate 

1.32for Minnesota businesses to refer to Taiwan as Taiwan in conducting their business
         with Taiwan; 

1.33and

1.34WHEREAS, the first agreement under the US-Taiwan Initiative on 21st Century Trade 

1.35negotiations officially came into effect on December 10, 2024. Negotiations regarding
         a second 

1.36agreement for a Bilateral Trade Agreement, the Avoidance of Double Taxation Agreement,
         and 

1.37the Indo-Pacific Economic Framework for Prosperity between Taiwan and the United States
         are 

1.38still ongoing. The agreements are important steps toward further strengthening bilateral
         trade 

1.39between our countries, thereby increasing Minnesota's exports to Taiwan and creating
         bilateral 

1.40investment and technical collaboration through tariff reduction and other trade facilitation
         measures; 

1.41and

1.42WHEREAS, Taiwan has undertaken a policy of "Integrated Diplomacy," highlighting 

1.43value-based diplomacy, alliance-based diplomacy, and economic and trade diplomacy,
         and Taiwan 

1.44is capable of, and willing to, fulfill its responsibilities and to collaborate with
         the world to deal with 

1.45the challenges of humanitarian aid and disease control; and

2.1WHEREAS, during this year's Asian American, Native Hawaiian, and Pacific Islander 

2.2Month, we celebrate Minnesota's vibrant Taiwanese American community that has enriched
         the 

2.3state's culture, cuisine, and commerce for decades; and

2.4NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it 

2.5that it reaffirms its commitment to strengthening and deepening the sister ties between
         the State of 

2.6Minnesota and Taiwan.

2.7BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED by the Senate of the State of Minnesota that it reaffirms 

2.8its support for the Taiwan Relations Act and encourages Minnesota businesses to refer
         to Taiwan 

2.9as Taiwan.

2.10BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that Minnesota endorses Taiwan's efforts to secure the 

2.11signing of a Bilateral Trade Agreement, the Avoidance of Double Taxation Agreement,
         and the 

2.12Indo-Pacific Economic Framework for Prosperity with the United States and reiterates
         its support 

2.13for a closer economic and trade partnership between the State of Minnesota and Taiwan.
